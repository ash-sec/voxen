## What Actually Goes Into a Strong Personal Brand on LinkedIn

Look, it's not mysterious, but it does require showing up regularly. Here's what tends to work for Sydney professionals:

**A profile that does the heavy lifting.** Your headline shouldn't just say your job title. It should say what you do and who you help. Your about section should read like a human wrote it, because ideally one did.

**Content that shares a perspective.** Not "excited to announce" posts. Not company press releases reformatted. Actual takes. Opinions. Stories from your week that have a point to them.

**Consistency over intensity.** Posting three times in one week and then vanishing for two months does almost nothing for your LinkedIn growth. The algorithm rewards regularity. So does your audience.

**Comments and conversations.** Personal branding isn't a broadcast. Leaving a thoughtful comment on someone else's post is sometimes more valuable than writing your own.

The professionals I see building genuine authority on LinkedIn in Sydney are the ones treating it like a slow build, not a campaign. They're showing up even when the post only gets eleven likes. Because their next client doesn't care about the likes. They care that the post existed.
