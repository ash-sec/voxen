## Is LinkedIn Ghostwriting Ethical? Let's Actually Answer That

Here's the direct answer: yes, LinkedIn ghostwriting is ethical because you are still providing the ideas, the expertise, the opinions, and the direction. The ghostwriter is a translator, turning your knowledge into words that land well. You are not deceiving anyone about your credentials, your experience, or your views.

The content reflects you. That's what matters.

LinkedIn itself doesn't prohibit ghostwriting. There's no rule, no policy, no hidden terms-of-service clause that says every post must be typed by the profile owner. The platform cares about authentic professional content, not whether you personally crafted each sentence at 11pm after a long day.

## Where It Could Get Dodgy (And How to Avoid That)

There are a few situations where LinkedIn ghostwriting *could* become ethically murky, and it's worth being honest about them.

**If the content directly contradicts your actual views.** If your ghostwriter is publishing takes you fundamentally disagree with just to chase engagement, that's a problem. Not because ghostwriting is wrong, but because you're misrepresenting yourself. Good done for you LinkedIn content is built around your real perspective, not invented ones.

**If you're in a regulated profession making specific claims.** Financial advisers, mortgage brokers, lawyers. If your LinkedIn content makes claims about advice or outcomes, you're responsible for those claims regardless of who wrote them. A decent LinkedIn ghostwriter will always flag this and make sure the content is compliant. This is especially relevant for LinkedIn content in Australia given ASIC guidelines and other professional standards.

**If you're engaging with comments you don't understand.** This one's less common but worth mentioning. If someone replies to a post and you have no idea what the post was actually about, that's when things feel dishonest. Stay across what goes out under your name. Simple.

Avoid those three things and you're completely in the clear.
