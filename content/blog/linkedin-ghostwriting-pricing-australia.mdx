## What LinkedIn Ghostwriting Pricing in Australia Usually Looks Like

There's no official industry rate. Honestly, that makes things both confusing and kind of interesting.

On the lower end, you'll find freelancers on platforms like Upwork or Fiverr charging anywhere from $15 to $50 per post. Some are decent. A lot of them are not writing for a specifically Australian professional audience, and it shows.

Mid-range LinkedIn ghostwriting services in Australia typically sit between $300 and $800 per month for a consistent posting schedule, usually two to four posts per week. This is where most business owners land when they want something properly done.

At the premium end, full-service LinkedIn content creation Australia packages can hit $1,500 to $3,000+ per month. These often include strategy, profile optimisation, engagement management, and sometimes DM outreach support.

## H2: What Are You Actually Paying For With LinkedIn Ghostwriting?

This is where a lot of people get caught out. They compare price tags without comparing what's actually in the package.

A genuinely good LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia will spend time learning how you think, how you speak, what your opinions are on your industry. That onboarding process takes effort. It's not just filling in a template.

Here's roughly what quality LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia pricing usually includes:

**Discovery and voice profiling.** A decent ghostwriter will interview you, review your existing content, and build out a profile of how you communicate. This alone can take two to three hours upfront.

**Content calendar and strategy.** Not just "three posts a week" but actually thinking about what kind of content serves your goals. Thought leadership? Lead generation? Building credibility in your niche?

**Writing, editing, and scheduling.** The actual posts, formatted for LinkedIn, written in your voice, ready to go.

**Revisions.** Most reputable services include at least one round of edits per post. If they don't, that's worth asking about.

Some packages also include monthly performance check-ins, engagement support, or LinkedIn profile rewrites. Others are purely done for you LinkedIn content, delivered to your inbox, nothing else. Both are valid. Just know what you're getting.

## H2: How to Evaluate LinkedIn Ghostwriting Pricing Without Getting Burned

A few practical things worth asking before you commit:

**Do they have samples or case studies?** Not just testimonials, actual writing samples. Read them. Do they sound like a real person or a press release?

**How do they capture your voice?** If the answer is "we'll send you a questionnaire," that's not necessarily a red flag, but dig deeper. What kind of questions? How much back and forth is involved?

**What's the revision process?** Unlimited revisions sounds great until it's being used to mask the fact that the first draft is always terrible.

**Are they familiar with your industry?** LinkedIn for financial advisers, LinkedIn for real estate agents, LinkedIn for mortgage brokers — these all require pretty different tones and content angles. A generalist can do okay, but someone who knows your world will do better.

**What's the contract length?** Month-to-month is lower risk. Six or twelve month contracts are fine if you've already seen their work and trust the relationship.
