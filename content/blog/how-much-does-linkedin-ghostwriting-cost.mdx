Pricing for LinkedIn ghostwriting is all over the place. Honestly, it can feel like asking how long a piece of string is, except the string is somehow charging you $500 a month and the other string wants $5,000.

So let's cut through it.

If you're trying to figure out how much LinkedIn ghostwriting costs before committing to anything, this is the breakdown you actually need.

## The Short Answer First

LinkedIn ghostwriting typically costs anywhere from **$300 to $5,000+ per month**, depending on who's doing it, how many posts you need, and what's included. Freelancers on the lower end, boutique agencies in the middle, premium personal branding firms at the top.

That range is enormous, yeah. Which is exactly why understanding *what* you're paying for matters more than the number itself.

## What Affects LinkedIn Ghostwriting Pricing?

### Volume of Posts

Most ghostwriters price based on how many posts they're delivering each week or month. Three posts a week is the sweet spot most professionals go with because it's enough to stay visible without overwhelming your audience.

One post a week. Cheaper. But you'll barely move the needle on LinkedIn's algorithm.

Five or more posts a week. More expensive, and honestly, probably more than most busy professionals actually need.

### The Writer's Experience Level

A freelancer fresh off Fiverr charging $30 a post isn't going to sound like you. Full stop.

Experienced LinkedIn ghostwriters who understand positioning, hook writing, and how the platform actually distributes content, those people charge more. Usually $150 to $400 per post at minimum, sometimes significantly more if they've built a personal brand themselves and genuinely understand what drives engagement.

### Strategy and Onboarding

Some ghostwriting services just write. They take a brief, produce content, done.

Others do a proper deep-dive into your voice, your industry, your goals, your audience. They'll nail your tone so well that your colleagues won't believe someone else wrote it. That extra layer of strategy and research costs more upfront but saves a painful amount of back-and-forth later.

### Revisions and Communication

Unlimited revisions sound great until you realise the hourly maths makes a writer want to cry. Services that offer structured revision rounds, usually one or two per post, tend to deliver better quality work because the writer knows exactly what they're signing up for. That structure usually gets priced in properly.

## Rough Pricing Tiers to Know About

**Budget end ($300 to $800/month):** Usually one to two posts per week from a generalist writer. Not necessarily bad, but you'll often feel like the content could belong to anyone. Minimal strategy involved.

**Mid-range ($800 to $2,500/month):** This is where most quality ghostwriting services sit. Three posts a week, some level of voice matching, basic LinkedIn strategy included. Good for professionals who want consistency without the premium agency price tag.

**Premium ($2,500 to $5,000+/month):** Often includes a dedicated writer, full brand strategy, content repurposing, and sometimes LinkedIn profile optimisation on top. Makes sense if you're a founder, executive, or speaker where personal brand is directly tied to revenue.

## Is LinkedIn Ghostwriting Actually Worth the Money?

Look, it depends entirely on what your time is worth and what LinkedIn means for your business.

If you're a consultant, executive, or business owner and one new client from LinkedIn pays for six months of ghostwriting, the maths makes itself. The question isn't really whether ghostwriting costs too much, it's whether your LinkedIn presence is costing you *more* by staying quiet.

Most professionals who try to write their own content end up doing one of two things. They post sporadically when they feel inspired, which is roughly never. Or they write posts that feel stiff and corporate because they're thinking too hard about how they sound instead of what they actually want to say.

A good ghostwriter solves both problems. Consistent content, in your actual voice, without you spending Sunday nights staring at a blinking cursor.

## What to Watch Out For When Hiring a LinkedIn Ghostwriter

Cheap isn't always a bargain. A writer who doesn't understand LinkedIn's algorithm, hook structure, or how to write for professional audiences will produce content that simply sits there collecting digital dust.

Ask these before you sign anything:

- Can I see examples of LinkedIn content you've written? (Not blog posts. LinkedIn posts specifically.)
- How do you capture someone's voice?
- What's included in your revision process?
- Do you have experience in my industry or a similar one?

Also, be a bit wary of anyone who promises viral posts or specific follower growth numbers. Good ghostwriters promise quality, consistency and genuine representation of your voice. Virality is a bonus, not a guarantee.

Some signs it might be time:

You've been meaning to post on LinkedIn for three months and haven't started. You know it matters for your business but you just can't make yourself sit down and write. Or you've been posting but getting zero traction because the content isn't quite landing.

Any of those feel familiar? That's your sign.
