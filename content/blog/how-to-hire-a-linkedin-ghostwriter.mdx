## What to Look for When You Hire a LinkedIn Ghostwriter

The first thing most people get wrong is treating this like hiring a copywriter for a landing page. It's not the same gig.

A LinkedIn ghostwriter needs to understand your voice, your industry, and the specific way professionals on LinkedIn engage with content. Generic writing gets ignored. Writing that sounds authentically like *you* gets shared.

When you're evaluating someone, ask to see LinkedIn-specific samples. Not blog posts. Not newsletters. Actual LinkedIn posts, ideally from accounts in your industry or at least in a similar professional space. LinkedIn content has its own rhythm, and not every writer gets it.

A few other things worth checking:

- Do they ask you about your audience, or do they just start writing?
- Can they explain why certain post formats work better than others?
- Do they understand the difference between thought leadership and promotional content?

If they can't answer those questions confidently, keep looking.

---

## H2: The Onboarding Process Matters More Than You Think

Good LinkedIn ghostwriting isn't a "send me your resume and I'll figure it out" situation. The onboarding process is where the magic either happens or doesn't.

A quality ghostwriter will want to understand your tone, your opinions, your career backstory, and the kinds of things you'd never say publicly. That last one is particularly useful. Knowing your limits helps them write content that actually sounds like you and not a LinkedIn influencer account you secretly find a bit cringe.

Expect a voice discovery call, or at minimum a detailed questionnaire. Some writers use interviews recorded on Loom or Zoom so they can hear how you actually talk. That's a good sign.

If someone promises to start writing immediately without any of this? Red flag. Huge one.

## H2: LinkedIn Ghostwriting Australia Has Its Own Flavour

This is worth saying plainly. Australian professionals communicate differently to Americans and Brits on LinkedIn.

We're generally less comfortable with overt self-promotion. We tend to lead with the story before the lesson. We use humour more readily, and we're often a bit suspicious of anyone who sounds too polished. LinkedIn personal branding in Australia works best when it feels genuine and a little bit human, not like a motivational poster.

According to LinkedIn's own data, Australia has one of the highest engagement rates per user in the Asia-Pacific region. Professionals here are active. The opportunity is real. But the content needs to fit the culture.

When you're hiring, it helps to work with someone who understands this. A LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia-based or at least deeply familiar with the Australian market will get this intuitively. Someone writing from the other side of the world might not.

## H2: Pricing, Contracts and What "Done for You LinkedIn Content" Actually Includes

Pricing varies wildly. You might see packages ranging from a couple hundred dollars a month to several thousand. Here's a rough guide to what you're actually buying at different price points.

**Budget end (under $500/month):** Usually 2 to 4 posts per month, minimal strategy, often templated. Can work if you're just starting out, but don't expect much personalisation.

**Mid-range ($500 to $1,500/month):** This is where genuine done for you LinkedIn content starts to make sense. You're getting regular posting, some strategy, and a writer who's actually engaged with your brand.

**Premium ($1,500+/month):** Full strategy, consistent posting schedule, engagement support, sometimes profile optimisation. Worth it if LinkedIn is a primary lead generation channel for your business.

Whatever the price point, make sure the contract is clear on a few things: who owns the content, what the revision process looks like, and what happens if the relationship isn't working.

Monthly rolling contracts are generally safer than locking into 6 months upfront with someone you haven't tested yet.

## H2: How to Test a LinkedIn Ghostwriter Before You Commit

Don't sign a long contract without a trial period. Seriously. A paid trial of 2 to 4 weeks tells you everything you need to know.

During the trial, pay attention to:

- How quickly they pick up your voice
- Whether they ask smart follow-up questions or just disappear and deliver
- How the content performs (even in a few weeks, you'll see engagement signals)
- How easy the revision process is

Some friction is normal early on. Voice matching takes time. But if the first draft sounds nothing like you and they argue when you give feedback, that's not a relationship you want to be in for six months.

LinkedIn content creation Australia is a growing industry, which means there are a lot of options. Take your time finding the right one.
