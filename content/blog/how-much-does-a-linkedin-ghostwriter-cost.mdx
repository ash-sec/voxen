Pricing for LinkedIn ghostwriters is all over the place. Like, genuinely all over the place. You can find someone charging $50 a month on Fiverr and a boutique agency quoting $5,000. Same service name, wildly different realities.

So what's actually reasonable? And more importantly, what are you actually paying for?

A LinkedIn ghostwriter in Australia typically costs anywhere from $300 to $3,000+ per month depending on experience, output volume, and how involved the process is. ## What LinkedIn Ghostwriter Costs Look Like in Australia

Let's talk real numbers. Not the polished "starting from" marketing speak, but what you'll actually encounter when you go looking.

**Freelancers and solo writers:** $200 to $800 per month. You might get two or three posts a week, but turnaround times vary, quality is inconsistent, and you're often managing the relationship yourself. Some are brilliant. Some will ghost you after week two.

**Mid-tier LinkedIn ghostwriting services:** $600 to $1,800 per month. This is where most established LinkedIn content creation Australia providers sit. You're getting a defined process, regular delivery, usually some kind of strategy or onboarding call, and someone who actually understands how the platform works.

**Premium agencies and consultants:** $2,000 to $5,000+ per month. These typically include content strategy, personal branding work, profile optimisation, analytics reporting, and sometimes paid amplification support. Useful if you're a C-suite exec or building a serious thought leadership presence from scratch.

## What You're Actually Paying For (It's Not Just Words)

Here's something most people miss. When you hire a LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia, you're not just buying words. You're buying time, expertise, and consistency, three things that are genuinely hard to manufacture on your own when you've got a business to run.

Good LinkedIn content Australia requires someone who understands the algorithm (which changes constantly), knows how to write hooks that stop the scroll, can capture your voice authentically, and posts with the kind of regularity that actually builds an audience.

That last bit matters more than people realise. Showing up twice a week for six months beats showing up brilliantly once and then disappearing for eight weeks. Every time.

A ghostwriter handles all of that. You show up for maybe an hour a month, if that, and your LinkedIn presence keeps ticking along without you having to think about it.

## Why Cheap Usually Costs More in the Long Run

Look, I get it. $1,200 a month feels like a lot if you've never invested in LinkedIn personal branding Australia before. But consider what happens with the budget options.

You spend three months with someone who doesn't quite get your voice. The posts go up, they look fine, they get basically no engagement. You cancel. You try again with someone else. Another three months. Same result.

Nine months later you've spent maybe $1,800 total and your LinkedIn still looks like a digital business card no one visits.

Done for you LinkedIn content only works when it's done well. Cheap execution on a platform as competitive as LinkedIn isn't a saving, it's a delay.

The professionals who price higher aren't being greedy. They've usually spent years learning what actually moves the needle on LinkedIn, and that knowledge has real commercial value.

## How to Evaluate Whether a Service Is Worth the Price

Not all LinkedIn ghostwriting services are equal, obviously. Here's what I'd look for before handing over any money.

**Voice matching.** Can they write in a way that genuinely sounds like you? Ask for samples from similar clients, not just a portfolio of their best generic work.

**Platform fluency.** Do they actually understand LinkedIn growth Australia and how the feed algorithm rewards certain content formats? If they're pitching you blog reposts and article links as your main content, that's a red flag.

**Process clarity.** How do they capture your ideas and expertise? A good service has a system, whether that's a monthly call, a voice memo workflow, or a questionnaire. Vague processes produce vague content.

**Realistic expectations.** Anyone promising 10,000 followers in 90 days is selling you something. Real LinkedIn growth Australia takes six to twelve months of consistent effort to show meaningful results.
