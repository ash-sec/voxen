## The "But Will It Sound Like Me?" Question

Every entrepreneur asks this. Fair question.

The answer depends entirely on the process. Lazy ghostwriting sounds generic. It reads like it was written for anyone, which means it resonates with no one. You can spot it immediately because it's all vague motivation and zero specifics.

Good LinkedIn content creation Australia-style means building a proper voice guide first. What words do you actually use? What do you care about? What opinions do you hold that most people in your industry would push back on? What stories do you have that nobody else could have?

That specificity is what makes content land. A mortgage broker talking about the exact conversation they had with a first-home buyer in Parramatta last Tuesday. A marketing consultant explaining the specific mistake they made with a client's campaign in 2022. Real details. Actual texture.

If a ghostwriter can nail that, the content doesn't just sound like you. It's better than most things you'd write yourself, because it's you without the blank page dread and the second-guessing.
