## What Good Personal Branding on LinkedIn Actually Looks Like

Not inspirational quotes. Please, not inspirational quotes.

Real personal branding on LinkedIn means showing your thinking. Your opinions on things happening in your industry. The mistakes you've made and what they taught you. The client situation that surprised you. The question you get asked every single week.

It's specific. It's a little bit vulnerable. It sounds like a person, not a press release.

For Melbourne professionals specifically, there's a real opportunity to tap into local context. The Melbourne property market, the CBD business environment, the particular flavour of industries here. LinkedIn content Australia that speaks to local realities tends to perform better because it resonates with the right audience rather than trying to appeal to everyone and landing with no one.

Frequency matters too. According to LinkedIn's own data, members who post weekly see 5x more profile views than those who don't. Five times. That's not a small edge, that's a completely different league of visibility.
