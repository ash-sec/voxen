## What LinkedIn Ghostwriting Actually Includes (and What It Does Not)

Before you can sensibly compare pricing, you need to know what you are actually comparing. This trips people up constantly.

LinkedIn ghostwriting is not just writing posts. At its most basic, yes, someone writes content that sounds like you and you post it. But the scope of what sits behind that varies enormously between providers.

### The Core Deliverables Most Services Offer

A solid LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia will typically include a discovery or onboarding process where a writer learns your voice, your industry, your opinions and your goals. Without that, the posts will read like they came from a generic content mill. Because they did.

From there, the service should include regular content creation, usually somewhere between two and five posts per week. It might also include strategy, meaning someone is thinking about what topics to cover, what angles to take, and how to build your audience over time. Some services include engagement support, which means responding to comments in your voice. Others include profile optimisation as part of the onboarding.

What LinkedIn ghostwriting typically does not include: paid advertising management, LinkedIn Sales Navigator training, lead generation outreach, or anything that sounds like a full marketing retainer. If a provider is bundling all of that together, you are paying for a lot more than ghostwriting and the price will reflect it.

The point is, when someone quotes you $500 a month and someone else quotes you $5,000, they are not necessarily delivering the same thing. Comparing them without understanding scope is like comparing a sandwich to a three-course meal because they both count as lunch.

## How Much Does LinkedIn Ghostwriting Cost: The Full Pricing Breakdown

### Tier Three: Premium Services ($2,000 to $5,000 per month)

Here you are often getting a senior writer or strategist, more frequent content, engagement management, and potentially profile work or broader LinkedIn content strategy included. Some premium providers also include regular strategy calls, analytics reporting, and content planning sessions.

This tier makes sense for C-suite executives, public figures, or founders who are heavily invested in LinkedIn as a primary business development channel.

### Tier Four: Agency Retainers ($5,000 plus per month)

Full-service agencies that bundle LinkedIn into a broader social and content strategy. Often overkill for most individual professionals, but relevant for larger businesses with multiple spokespeople or a significant content marketing budget.

## Freelancers vs Agencies vs Done For You LinkedIn Content Services

The structure of who you hire matters as much as how much you pay. These three models work very differently in practice.

### Freelancers

Hiring a freelance LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia can work brilliantly or terribly, often depending on luck and how good you are at vetting people. A strong freelancer who specialises in LinkedIn content Australia will know the platform, write well, and be genuinely invested in your results.

The risk? Availability. A freelancer juggling multiple clients might be slow to respond, inconsistent with delivery, or simply unavailable when you need a quick turnaround. There is also no team behind them if life gets in the way.

Expect to pay anywhere from $50 to $250 per post for a quality Australian freelancer, or a monthly retainer in the $800 to $2,500 range depending on their experience and output.

Full-service content or marketing agencies often offer LinkedIn as part of a broader retainer. Pricing is higher, the team is larger, and you might get more robust strategy and reporting.

The downside is that your account might get handed to a junior writer while you pay senior rates. That happens more often than agencies would like to admit. Also, for LinkedIn personal branding Australia specifically, you want someone who genuinely understands the platform's culture and content style. Not every agency does.

## Red Flags to Watch For When Hiring a LinkedIn Ghostwriter Australia

Look, not everyone offering LinkedIn ghostwriting in Australia knows what they are doing. Here are the warning signs worth paying attention to.

### They Cannot Show You Samples That Sound Different from Each Other

A good LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia writes in different voices for different clients. If every sample in their portfolio reads the same way, they are not adapting to individual voices. They are publishing their own voice under different names. That is not ghostwriting.

### They Promise Specific Follower Growth Numbers

Follower growth on LinkedIn depends on a lot of variables: how often you engage, your industry, your network size, whether your content gets shared, and frankly, some element of unpredictability. Any service guaranteeing 500 new followers in 30 days is either misleading you or planning to use tactics that violate LinkedIn's terms of service.

### No Onboarding Process

If a provider is ready to start writing for you before they have asked you a single question about your opinions, your clients, your goals or your voice, walk away. Good LinkedIn content Australia starts with genuine understanding, not templates.

### They Do Not Use LinkedIn Themselves

Fair enough if a copywriter focuses on other platforms. But if someone is selling LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia and their own LinkedIn profile has three posts from 2022, that tells you something.

### Vague or Confusing Pricing

Pricing should be clear. If you cannot work out what you are paying for and when, that ambiguity tends to work in the provider's favour, not yours.
