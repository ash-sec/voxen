## The Five Things to Look For When Finding a LinkedIn Ghostwriter

Right. So how to find a LinkedIn ghostwriter who is actually worth hiring. Here are the five things that matter most.

### 1. They Have a Discovery Process

Any LinkedIn ghostwriter worth their rate will want to understand you before they write a single word. They will ask about your business goals, your target audience, your opinions on industry topics, your communication style and what you want LinkedIn to do for you. If someone offers to just start writing with minimal onboarding, that is a problem.

The discovery process is where your voice gets captured. Without it, you get generic content. With it, you get posts that could only have come from you.

### 2. They Can Show You Samples in Different Voices

Ask to see examples from two or three different clients. If every sample sounds the same, that writer has one voice. Their voice. And that is what you will get, regardless of what they promise about personalisation.

LinkedIn content creation Australia is not a one-size-fits-all job. A financial adviser in Perth needs to sound completely different from a tradie building a referral network in Brisbane.

### 3. They Understand Your Industry (Or Are Willing to Learn It Properly)

You do not need a ghostwriter who is an expert in your exact field. But you do need one who is intellectually curious, asks smart questions and does their research. A consultant who advises on supply chain logistics has a very different LinkedIn audience than a business coach targeting small business owners.

Your ghostwriter needs to understand that distinction and write accordingly.

### 4. They Have a Clear Revision Policy

Things will not be perfect immediately. That is normal. The first month of any LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia relationship involves calibration. Your ghostwriter writes, you give feedback, they adjust. A good service has a clear process for this and does not charge extra for reasonable edits.

Be wary of anyone who sends you content and takes feedback as a personal affront.

### 5. They Post Consistently (Not Just Occasionally)

Ask how many posts they produce per week or month and what the delivery process looks like. Consistency is the entire point. One great post a month is not a LinkedIn content strategy Australia. Three solid posts a week, consistently, over six months, is how real results get built.

If a ghostwriter cannot commit to a regular delivery schedule, keep looking.

## Red Flags That Should Send You Running

Look, not every LinkedIn ghostwriter in Australia is the real deal. Some are genuinely bad at writing. Some are good writers but terrible at capturing voice. Some are just content mills repackaging the same templates with your name swapped in.

Here are the warning signs.

### They Cannot Explain Their Onboarding Process

If you ask "how do you capture my voice?" and the answer is vague or focuses entirely on things like "I research your industry" without any mention of talking to you directly, that is a concern. Voice capture requires direct conversation. Full stop.

### They Promise Viral Results

Nobody can promise virality. LinkedIn growth Australia comes from consistency and relevance, not from any single magic post. Anyone who tells you they can get you 10,000 followers in 90 days is selling you something that almost certainly will not deliver.

### Their Own LinkedIn Is Quiet or Generic

This one is interesting. Some ghostwriters are brilliant at writing for others but have terrible personal LinkedIn presence. Fair enough, they are busy writing for clients. But if their profile reads like a resume from 2014 and their content sounds like a motivational calendar, that tells you something.

### They Cannot Give You a Client Reference

Any legitimate LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia should be able to point you to at least one client who is happy to speak with you. They might keep names confidential publicly, which is understandable given the nature of ghostwriting, but a direct reference should be possible if you ask.

### Everything Is Locked in a Long-Term Contract Immediately

It is reasonable to ask for a minimum commitment of a month or two. But being pushed into a 12-month contract before you have seen a single piece of content is a big red flag. You want to see the work first.

## How to Brief a Ghostwriter So They Actually Sound Like You

This is where a lot of professional relationships fall apart, and it is usually not the ghostwriter's fault.

The briefing process is a skill. Most people have never had to articulate their own voice to another person before, so they give vague answers to onboarding questions and then wonder why the content sounds off.

### What to Include in Your Brief

Be specific about your opinions. Not "I think good customer service matters" but "I think most businesses have completely misunderstood what customer service actually means, and here is why." Specific, opinionated, a bit provocative. That is what makes LinkedIn content Australia worth reading.

Tell your ghostwriter what you would never say. This is just as important. If there is a common take in your industry that you think is rubbish, say so. If you would never use certain words or phrases, flag them. The negative space is just as useful as the positive.

### Give Them Raw Material

The best thing you can give a LinkedIn ghostwriter is your unfiltered thinking. Voice notes on your commute. Old emails where you explained something to a client really well. Past talks or presentations you have given. Social media posts from your personal accounts where you were being yourself.

Raw material is gold. The ghostwriter's job is to shape it, not to invent you from scratch.

### Check In Regularly

A monthly call, even just 20 minutes, makes a big difference. Talking through what is happening in your world, what you are thinking about, what clients are asking you lately, that keeps the content fresh and genuinely reflective of where you are at right now.

LinkedIn content strategy Australia that is built on live, current thinking will always outperform recycled ideas.

This sounds obvious but it works. If you know someone whose LinkedIn content you genuinely admire, reach out and ask who writes for them. Referrals in this space are valuable because you can see the actual output before you make a decision.

A bit meta, but it works. Search "LinkedIn ghostwriter Australia" on LinkedIn and look at the profiles that come up. Do their profiles sound good? Is their content interesting and well-written? Are they posting consistently themselves? All of that is useful evidence.

Any serious LinkedIn ghostwriting service Australia should be able to show you work. Even if client names are kept confidential, there should be examples of different voices, different industries and different content styles. If the portfolio all looks the same, the writer has one mode.
