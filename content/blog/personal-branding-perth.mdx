## What Personal Branding in Perth Actually Looks Like in Practice

Look, there's a lot of vague advice floating around about "sharing your story" and "adding value." Helpful. Cheers.

Here's what it actually means for a busy Perth professional.

**It means posting regularly.** Not every day, not with some elaborate content calendar. But consistently enough that when your name comes up in conversation, someone can say "yeah, I follow them on LinkedIn." Three times a week is a reasonable rhythm for most people.

**It means talking about the specific work you do.** Not generic industry hot takes lifted from a Forbes article. The deal that nearly fell apart and how you fixed it. The client problem no one else had thought to solve. The decision you made last month that you'd make differently now.

**It means having a point of view.** Perth's business community respects directness. Say what you actually think.

LinkedIn content creation in Australia has exploded over the last couple of years, but genuinely useful, specific, locally relevant content is still rare. That's your opening.

## The Biggest Mistake Perth Professionals Make With Their Personal Brand

They wait until they need it.

Personal branding isn't something you build in a hurry when business slows down or you're about to launch something new. It takes months of consistent presence before the momentum becomes self-sustaining. The professionals who have strong LinkedIn followings in Perth today started posting when it felt pointless, when they had twelve followers and their mum was the only one liking their posts.

The other mistake? Treating LinkedIn like a resume. Just listing achievements and credentials without giving anyone a reason to keep reading.

LinkedIn growth in Australia rewards generosity, sharing what you know, giving people something useful, letting your personality come through. The algorithm likes it. More importantly, real humans like it.
