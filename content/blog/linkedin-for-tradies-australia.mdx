## What to Actually Post on LinkedIn as a Tradie

This is where most people freeze up. "I'm not a writer. I pull cable for a living. What am I supposed to say?"

More than you think, actually.

Some of the best-performing LinkedIn content Australia has to offer comes from people who just talk plainly about what they do. A sparky posting about why dodgy switchboard work is a fire hazard. A plumber explaining what actually causes blocked drains. A builder walking through what happens when waterproofing is rushed.

That's genuinely useful stuff. People read it, share it, and remember who wrote it.

A few content angles that work well for tradies:

- **Behind the scenes of a job** (photos, short explanations, the before and after)
- **Myth-busting posts** about your trade, stuff clients get wrong all the time
- **Lessons from a difficult project** without throwing anyone under the bus
- **What to look for when hiring a tradie** in your specific trade
- **Local industry observations**, changes in materials, regulations, labour costs

The key is consistency. Posting once and going quiet doesn't build anything. LinkedIn personal branding Australia works the same way a reputation does in real life, it compounds over time with steady effort.

## Setting Up Your LinkedIn Profile So It Actually Does Something

Your profile is your shopfront. If someone lands on it after reading one of your posts, it needs to do some work.

A few things worth sorting out before you start posting:

**Your headline.** Don't just write your job title. Write who you help and what outcome you deliver. "Licensed Electrician | Commercial & Residential Fit-Outs Across Greater Sydney" is a lot more useful than "Electrician at XYZ Electrical."

**Your About section.** Talk to your ideal client directly. What problems do you solve? What makes your operation different? How long have you been at it? Keep it plain-spoken, no corporate fluff.

**Social proof.** Recommendations from past clients and project managers carry serious weight. Ask for them. Most people are happy to write one if you make it easy.

**Contact details.** Sounds obvious. You'd be amazed how many profiles have no phone number, no email, no website. Make it dead simple to reach you.

## The Consistency Problem (And How to Get Around It)

Here's the honest challenge. You're running a trade business. You're on tools, quoting jobs, managing subbies, chasing invoices. Sitting down to write LinkedIn posts on a Thursday night is not happening.

A few practical options:

**Batch your content.** Block out an hour on Sunday, record voice memos of ideas from the week, turn those into posts. Rough notes work fine.
