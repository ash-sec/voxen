## LinkedIn Tips for Business Owners: Start With Your Profile, Not Your Posts

Here's the thing most people skip. They obsess over content strategy before their profile even passes a basic credibility check.

Your LinkedIn profile is a landing page. Full stop.

When someone reads one of your posts and clicks through, they're deciding in about four seconds whether to follow you or bounce. A blurry headshot, a vague headline like "Director at XYZ Pty Ltd", and a summary that reads like a resume from 2011? That's a hard bounce.

Fix these three things first:

**Headline.** Tell people what you do and who you help. "I help Sydney tradies win more commercial contracts" beats "Managing Director" every single time.

**Banner image.** Use it. A simple graphic with your service, your website, or even just your business name looks dramatically more professional than the default grey.

**About section.** Write it in first person. Tell a quick story. End with a clear call to action. LinkedIn personal branding Australia starts here, not in your feed.

## The LinkedIn Content Australia Formula That Actually Works

Alright, onto posting. The single most common mistake? Writing like a press release.

LinkedIn rewards posts that feel like they came from a human being who has opinions and experiences. Not corporate announcements. Not recycled industry news with a "Thoughts?" tacked on the end.

Posts that consistently perform well tend to follow a simple pattern: a strong opening line that earns the scroll, a short story or specific observation, and a clear takeaway. That's it.

According to LinkedIn's own data, posts written by individual people (not company pages) get significantly higher reach and engagement. Your personal profile is your biggest asset. Use it.

A few things that work well for business owners specifically:

- Stories from client work (anonymised if needed)
- Lessons you learned the hard way
- Opinions on things happening in your industry
- Behind-the-scenes of how your business actually runs
- Honest takes on what's changed in your market lately

Avoid: motivational quotes, vague thought leadership, and anything that sounds like it was written by a committee.

## LinkedIn for Business Owners: Engagement Is Not Optional

Posting and disappearing is like handing out business cards and walking away before anyone can read them.

LinkedIn's algorithm is pretty upfront about this: accounts that engage with others get more reach. Comment on posts from people in your industry. Reply to everyone who comments on your stuff. Send genuine connection requests with an actual note explaining why you want to connect.

Twenty minutes of real engagement a day beats three hours of perfecting a single post.

And look, it doesn't have to be deep and meaningful every time. Even a short, specific comment ("this matches exactly what we saw happen in our construction business last year") does more than a thumbs up.
